History

The brain-child of eccentric businessman James Arthur Donald, Uncanny Yarns began life as part of a complicated Ponzi-style investment scheme. Seeing the rising popularity of pulps as cheap entertainment, J. A. D. enlisted the help of his former Nanny, Marsha Williams, to create a fake magazine that he brought to several investors with promises of outlandish profits. He would then take the money he got from one investor and pay the next, leaving each financier with a copy of Uncanny Yarns completely written by J.A.D. and Mrs. Williams. Unfortunately for J. A. D., he forgot to skim any money from these transactions, resulting in the entire enterprise boiling down to unregulated money transfers between unaware parties.
